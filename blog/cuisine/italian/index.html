 Orange Liqueur Semi-Freddo
A boozy twist on a favourite of ours. We've bought a lot of frozen versions of this but it's actually quite easy (and cheaper) to make yourself! - We adapted this recipe from Rick Stein's Long Weekends book

Instructions
Meringue
  1. Assemble your ingredients
  2. Pre heat oven to 100C and line a rectangular baking sheet (approx. 24cm x 33xm ) with greaseproof
  1. Whisk the egg whites until you get soft peaks. Add the sugar a bit at a time until you have stiff peaks. Spread the meringue mix out flat onto your baking sheet
  2. Put this in the oven for around 2 hours. You want it to be quite firm so that you can cut it later
Filling
  1. Squeeze your orange juice ready and add the liqueur - this combination tastes great but if you don't have these ingredients in try making something else, another liqueur or some marsala wine will work well
  2. Whisk egg yolks and sugar in a large heatproof bowl (we will need to put this over a pan of hot water later). Whisk until the mix is thick and pale.
  3. Put the bowl over a pan of simmering water to make a bain marie, (if your bowl falls into the pan try putting another bowl upside down in the bottom of the pan to lift up your mixing bowl). Continue to whisk for around 5 minutes, the mix will start getting really thick like custard
  4. Add your liqueur mix a little at a time, whisking in between. When it's all added take off the heat and set to one side
  5. Whisk up the double cream until thick and then fold in the custardy mix
Assembly
  1. See the video for assembly (https://youtu.be/d9jYclDgMXU)
  2. Line your loaf tin with the cling film, use enough to cover the sides with enough left to fold over at the end
  3. Cut the meringue into 3 or 4 equal bits so that they will fit in the loaf tin
  4. Put one bit of meringue into the tin, then add 1/3 of the filling - repeat until you have used everything up - like making a lasagne!
  5. Cover it all in clingfilm and put in the freezer for a few hours to chill - this will make it much easier to cut, and it tastes great cold!
Recipe Notes

Try out different things in the filling and get creative - e.g. limoncello, marsala wine, fruit or whatever you might have around the house!
