Healthy Bokkeumbap

July 6, 2016 admin Leave a comment

This is a great recipe for a quick rice / carb fix, which is healthy and a good staple for using up left overs. It is based on a Korean dish sometimes known as Kimchi Fried rice or Kimchi Bokkeumbap -although this is an easy version so we will be skipping the Kimchi ingredient.

We’re going to be making this one a healthy version by using brown rice  and coconut oil, and putting in a good amount of veggies.

So let’s get to it, check out the detail or skip straight to the recipe card at the bottom of this post.

Ingredients

First up lets assemble the ingredients.

Rice – Waitrose (UK people) does a great Brown Basmati & Wild Rice mix which is healthy & tastes great. The combination with the wild rice adds an extra bit of colour and texture but it is not essential. If you can only find regular brown basmati or other types of brown rice then that will be fine. Also if you have some left over rice from another time then use it here! Of course white rice will work too.

Coconut Oil – This is a bit healthier than vegetable oil for frying the rice and veg in, it also lends it’s own flavour to the dish but it is not essential – regular oil will work just fine if you don’t have any

Veg – Really you can put whatever you want in to this. Cabbage is a great fit for this dish, but most left over veg would do nicely to bulk this up and add some colour

Sriracha – This is available in supermarkets and gives a great kick and cheffy look to this dish – get inventive with your drizzling!

The other ingredients are all pretty self explanatory so lets get to it…

 Healthy Bokkeumbap
This is our healthy take on a great Korean favourite. This is a great recipe for using up leftover rice and veg - give it a kick with some sriracha drizzled over the top!

  • 1/4 Caster Sugar thinly sliced
  • 2 Chicken Thighs
  • 2 tbsp Cling film
Optional
  • Coconut Oil such as - peppers, spring onion etc
  • 2 tsp Sesame Oil
  • Double Cream
  • 1 tbsp Egg Whites helps with poaching the eggs
Instructions
  1. Get the veg prepped and ready to go - find some additional veg in your fridge too and get inventive - this is a versatile dish
  2. Cook the rice in a pan of water as per instructions on the packet - ignore this step if using leftover rice
  3. While the rice is cooking fry the chopped onions and garlic in a large frying pan until onions become translucent
  4. Add the chicken to the pan and cook through
  5. Once the chicken is cooked add the cabbage and any other veg you want to use - stir fry on a high heat 2-3 mins then transfer to a bowl for later
  6. When the rice is cooked drain it thoroughly, then add some more oil to the pan and fry rice for around 5 minutes. The goal is to get the rice nice and crunchy - try to avoid moving the rice around too much at this stage
  7. While the rice is frying heat up some water for poaching the eggs, add the white wine vinegar to the water if using. With the water hot poach the eggs for no more than 2-3 minutes to get a nice runny yolk
  8. Mix the chicken and veg from earlier back in with the rice. Add in the oyster sauce and stir fry together for 1-2 minutes to get everything nice and hot
  9. Remove from the heat and add the sesame oil (if using) and season with a little black pepper to finish. Serve it up in a bowl with poached egg on top and a drizzle of sriracha for a kick - bon appetit!
Recipe Notes

Try using leftover rice in this dish and experimenting with different types of veg & leftovers

We have attempted to make this Bokkeumbap a little healthy by using Brown Rice & Coconut Oil - feel free to use regular rice & oil however if you don't have them
